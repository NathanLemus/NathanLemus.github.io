Intelligent Systems Club Autonomous Snowplow

Overview
Team Yeti is a team of the Intelligent Systems Club's competetive teams. Yeti X competed at the ION Autonomous Snowplow Competition. The competition page can be visited Here. The autonomous snowplow team started at UM Dearborn in 2010 by Dr. Narasimhamurthi Natarajan. Dr. "Nattu" passed away in 2016. Despite never meeting Dr. Nattu, his commitment to the team is something I wish to uncover for the new members joining the club well into the future.
For the 2020 iteration of the robot, the team wanted to clean up the mechanichal and electrical shortcommings. The code would be improved also to add more robustness and clarity to its functionality. Having joined shorthly after the 2019 competition season, which had ended in January of the same year, I was excited to fill the void of the electrical team. My mentor, team lead, and only other electrical member, David, was key in instilling my respect for electrical engineering. Although not my major, I had certianly been exposed to electrical engineering courses such as two circuits classes and physics II.

Design Goals and Challanges
The state of the electrical design when my partner and I inherited it was robust enough. Despite a very messy apperance, most of the functionality intendid for the system we ended up keeping moving fowards. The lack of documentation for the design was certianly a major aspect I worked on moving fowards.
